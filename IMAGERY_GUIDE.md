# Optimum Academy Imagery & Branding Guide

This guide outlines the imagery requirements and recommendations for the Optimum Academy website. Following these specifications ensures brand consistency, authenticity, and high conversion impact.

## Core Visual Identity
- **Professional & Trustworthy:** Clean, high-resolution, and well-lit.
- **Welcoming & Human-centered:** Focus on real people, diverse students, and genuine interactions.
- **Career-focused:** Show the transition from learning to the workforce (60/40 workplace-to-classroom ratio).

---

## Custom Imagery Requirements
The following sections require manual image sourcing or custom photography, as high-quality, authentic stock alternatives are unavailable.

### 1. Manual Tasks (Manual Handling)
- **Course:** `HLTWHS005 - Conduct manual tasks safely`
- **Orientation:** Landscape (16:9)
- **Dimensions:** Min 2000px width
- **Subject Matter:** A professional in a care or industrial setting demonstrating safe lifting techniques with a hoist, slide sheet, or colleague.
- **Why Custom:** Existing stock is often overly clinical, outdated, or generic "worker with box" imagery that lacks the specific care-sector context.

### 2. First Aid & CPR
- **Courses:** `HLTAID009` and `HLTAID011`
- **Orientation:** Landscape (16:9)
- **Dimensions:** Min 2000px width
- **Subject Matter:** Students or professionals practicing on adult/infant manikins in a modern training room. Avoid sterile, white-background "kit" photos. Focus on the *action* and *confidence* of the learner.
- **Why Custom:** Free stock is often low-quality or shows incorrect techniques/outdated equipment.

### 3. NDIS & Aged Care (Australian Context)
- **Section:** Course cards for Individual Support and Community Services.
- **Orientation:** Landscape (4:3 or 16:9)
- **Dimensions:** Min 1200px width
- **Subject Matter:** Real-world care settings in Australia. Diverse aged care or disability support workers interacting with clients in home-like environments.
- **Specifications:** Soft lighting, authentic clothing (not overly clinical scrubs), and visible human connection (e.g., sharing a laugh, a meal, or a conversation).

## Brand Consistency Checklist
- [ ] **No Overly Posed Corporate Imagery:** Avoid people pointing at screens or "thumbs up" poses.
- [ ] **Diverse Representation:** Include students and professionals of different ages, backgrounds, and abilities.
- [ ] **Australian Context:** Ensure uniforms, environments, and signage look consistent with the Australian workforce.
- [ ] **Consistent Color Palette:** Prioritize images that naturally incorporate brand purples, blues, or neutral soft tones.
